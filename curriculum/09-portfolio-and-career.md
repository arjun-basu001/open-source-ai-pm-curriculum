# 09 — Portfolio & Career

> **Goal:** Turn your learning into visible proof of capability that hiring managers and collaborators can evaluate quickly.

---

## The core idea

Course certificates are fine, but they don’t prove you can ship.
A strong AI PM portfolio shows three things clearly:

1. You can identify a meaningful problem
2. You can build and evaluate a solution
3. You can communicate trade-offs and risk like a professional

---

## The three artifacts that matter

### 1) AI Feature Strategy Brief (3–5 pages)
Include:
- Problem and user clarity
- Why AI is the right approach
- System and model choice rationale
- Risk analysis and mitigations
- Success metrics with thresholds
- Launch recommendation with go/no-go gates

### 2) Working prototype
Needs:
- Public URL
- Clean GitHub repo + readable README
- Prompts/system behavior documented
- Eval result summary
- Honest list of limitations

### 3) Evaluation + governance packet
Include:
- 25-case test set + rubric
- LLM-as-judge prompt + human spot-check comparison
- Failure taxonomy table
- 5-row risk register
- Copyright/IP policy paragraph (if third-party content involved)
- Rollout guardrails and escalation triggers

---

## Where to publish your work

- **GitHub:** treat repos as portfolio pages, not code dumps
- **Personal site / Notion:** one page linking all artifacts
- **LinkedIn:** share one concrete build/eval insight per post

Specificity beats hype every time.

## Exercises

**Exercise 1 — Portfolio audit**  
Pick your top 3 artifacts from this curriculum. If you only have 1–2 strong pieces, scope your next artifact in one paragraph.

**Exercise 2 — Strategy brief draft**  
Write the full brief and get one real review from a peer/mentor/hiring manager.

**Exercise 3 — GitHub cleanup**  
Improve README quality, add deployment links, and archive low-signal repos.

**Exercise 4 — One public write-up**  
Publish a 200–300 word post on one specific thing you built/evaluated.

---

## Exit criteria

- [ ] Three portfolio artifacts complete and publicly presentable
- [ ] Repos are clean, documented, and easy to navigate
- [ ] At least one public technical write-up published
- [ ] You can explain your technical choices and risk decisions confidently
- [ ] Your portfolio tells a coherent story about your AI PM profile
