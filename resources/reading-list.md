# Reading List

If you want to go beyond the core modules, start here.

Everything below is either free or has a meaningful free tier/access path.

---

## Foundations

- **[Attention Is All You Need](https://arxiv.org/abs/1706.03762)** — Original transformer paper.
- **[The Illustrated Transformer](https://jalammar.github.io/illustrated-transformer/)** — Best visual companion.
- **[Software 2.0 (Karpathy)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6aXx8I0Fx3Q)** — Short, still relevant.

## Product and strategy

- **[Stratechery](https://stratechery.com/)** — Ongoing analysis of AI market dynamics.
- **[Every — AI writing archive](https://every.to/chain-of-thought)** — Practical product thinking.
- **[Lenny’s Newsletter](https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/)** — Search AI archive for PM insights.

## Technical depth

- **[Eugene Yan writing](https://eugeneyan.com/writing/)** — Applied ML and LLM systems.
- **[Hamel Husain](https://hamel.dev/)** — Sharp writing on evals and applied AI engineering.
- **[Simon Willison](https://simonwillison.net/)** — Weekly high-signal updates.

## Safety and governance

- **[Anthropic Responsible Scaling Policy](https://www.anthropic.com/index/anthropics-responsible-scaling-policy)**
- **[NIST AI RMF Playbook](https://airc.nist.gov/AI_RMF_Knowledge_Base/Playbook)**
- **[Alan Turing Institute — Fairness, Privacy, Transparency](https://www.wipo.int/about-ip/en/frontier_technologies/ai_and_ip.html)**
- **[Partnership on AI resources](https://partnershiponai.org/resources/)**

## Books worth your time

- **Designing Machine Learning Systems** — Chip Huyen
- **Building LLMs for Production** — Louis-François Bouchard
- **The Alignment Problem** — Brian Christian
